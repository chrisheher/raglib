1.6 Machine Languages, Assembly Languages and High-level
Languages

Programmers write instructions in various programming languages, 
some directly understandable by the computer and others that require
 intermediate translation steps. Hundreds of computer languages are in use today. 

These may be divided into three general types:

1. Machine languages
2. Assembly languages
3. High-level languages.

Any computer can directly understand only its own machine language. 
Machine language is the “natural language” of a particular computer. 
It is defined by the hardware design of that computer. Machine languages 
generally consist of strings of numbers (ultimately reduced to 1s and Qs)
