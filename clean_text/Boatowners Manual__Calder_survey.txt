Check the raw-water circuit:

Check the pump’s exterior for signs of
water and/or oil leaks (Figure 9-52G).
Remove the raw-water pump cover
and check the impeller for missing
vanes, cracks, or excessive wear (the
impeller tips will be worn flat; Figure
9-52H).

Remove any zincs from the heat 
exchanger. If corroded to the point of
nonexistence or near nonexistence,
they have been neglected and expensive 
corrosion problems are possible.

Remove the end caps from the heat
exchanger and inspect the tube stack
for scaling, blockage, or corrosion.

Check for the existence of a vented
loop. If not present, the engine may
have flooded with salt water at some
point.
