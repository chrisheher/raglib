One of the poetic techniques that empowers Homer’s aesthetic is the
frequent use of the simile, In comparing one thing to another, one is
beginning to abstract certain features of one concrete system to compare
them to another. Consider Book II, lines 142-151:

One can appreciate why Jean Gebser, in his theories of the evolution of 
consciousness,° sees the Greeks as  foundational in the development of 
a new “Mental Structure” of consciousness. For the Greeks effected a shift 
from the Mythical to the Mental structure of consciousness. Whether it is
 Anaximander’s turning the myth of the Great Mother into the philosophical 
concept of the apeiron (the Unlimited), or Homer's formulating the protological 
syllogism in the form of the Homeric simile, we are encountering a new aesthetic in 
which both poetry and philosophy are performing the shift from one mentality to another. 
Since logic represents an abstract idealization of a concrete process, it is part and
parcel of a process of a Platonic idealization in which the intellectual
geometrical figure is promoted to a higher state of value than the merely
physical, sensible object. I

By the time of Plato, when a literate ruling class had been achieved in
Greek society, it was precisely the power of this poetic figure of cultural
authority that stood in the way of the philosopher. Plato wanted that
Homeric charisma and power for himself, and the unsettling complexity and
irony of the artist was disturbing for the ideologist who wished to hold
society in the grip of eternal and unchanging forms. The poet was too shifty I
and complex a character, and so Plato banished all poets from his ideal state.
‘This conflict between artist and ideologue is basic to literate civilization, with
its argument over whether writing should be used for art or propaganda,
Whether it is the execution of Lorca in Franco’s Spain or the death sentence
against Rushdi in the Ayatollah’s Iran, this battle rages on wherever
fundamentalist ideals — Platonic, Christian, Judaic, Islamic, or Marxist —
seek to reduce the complex ecology of human consciousness to a single and
linear ideology.
