If Lines Are Doubled

If two lines are used anywhere with the idea of getting
double the strength of one, they must be of equal length
when strain is put upon them. Otherwise one carries the
load first and parts, and then the other follows suit.

Two Lines on One Pile

If you must drop a line over a pile where there is already a 
similar line from another boat, it is a simple trick
to rig your line so that either can be removed without
disturbing the other. Take the eye of your line and run it
up through the first eye from below as shown in Figure
720, top.

In you find that another owner has dropped an eye over
yours ona pile or cleat without “dipping” it through your
eye, you can still get yours clear easily by reversing the
process. Get a little slack in the other line and slip your
eye up through its loop, and on over the top of the pile.
Your line then can be dropped through the eye of the
other, and you are clear.

If yours is to be the second line on the pile, it’s both
prudent and courteous to dip your line through the first
loop. The other owner may not know the trick for getting
his line clear without removing yours.
