Many businesses now provide Web sites containing shopping-cart applications, 
which allow customers to buy items conveniently on the Web. The sites record what the consumer
wants to purchase and provide an easy, understandable way to shop online. They do so by
using an electronic shopping cart, just as people would use physical shopping carts in retail
stores. Users can add or remove items from their shopping carts, and the sites will update
the contents automatically. When users “check out,” they pay for the items currently in their
shopping carts. To see a real-world electronic shopping cart, we suggest going to the online
bookstore Amazon.com (www.amazon. com).
