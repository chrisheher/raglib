My love,


Here we are, our plans made for us this time by a pretty ghastly breakdown
of the car. and so I can take the opportunity to write you rather more of a letter
than I have been able to manage in some time. And perhaps modify a few
things which have perhaps troubled you; coming as they have in peacemeal
sentences as bulletins on a consistent state of calamity.

Still I know what you are feeling under it all: even if there are occasional
concerns (I imagine that the story of the suitcase gave you rather a turn) it is
much better because things are happening, and moving, and alive, and not in
one corner of Greenwich Vill. —and as long as I am eating and sleeping &
everything is all right. Good. I feel just that way.

Washington, as you could gather, was a pretty messy business, chiefly

Mike’s floor, chiefly difficult because we were both so discouraged at being
stuck so near to NewYork, as if we might never get further. And so when we
could leave we streaked out for South Carolina, and stopped at Chapel Hill.
