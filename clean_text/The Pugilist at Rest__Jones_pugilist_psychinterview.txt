the simple-minded animal consciousness of the here-and-now. That
I had been a great hero of the circus — the dog shot from cannons,
the dog that dove from fifty-foot platforms into shallow barrels of
water, the dog that rode galloping stallions bareback — that I was
Boris, the Great One, a celebrated hero of Mother Russia, beloved
by my countrymen meant . . . nothing to me.
