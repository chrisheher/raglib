Links between sentences in a paragraph and between paragraphs are called 
transitions (from Latin words that mean going across). Transitions make your writing
flow smoothly and logically, so your reader doesn’t have to work too hard to
understand what you mean. If you make your reader work too hard, you'll lose
him. Good, clear writing is a demonstration of courtesy and consideration for
your reader. You are asking him to follow your line of thought and to agree with
you; any irritants, sloppiness, or lack of consideration for him will make him
want to stop reading, and you will lose a supporter. He can’t read your mind, so
he needs your basic thought, supported by specific examples of exactly what
you mean, presented in the clearest possible terms. Transitions help by linking
ideas in a logical way.

relationships between sentences and between paragraphs:
