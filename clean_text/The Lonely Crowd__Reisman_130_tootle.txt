There is, perhaps, a streak of similar realism in Tootle.
There the adults play the role we have described earlier:
they manipulate the child into conformity with the peer group 
and then reward him for the behavior for which they
have already set the stage. Moreover, the citizens of Engineville 
are tolerant of Tootle: they understand and do not get
indignant. And while they gang up on him with red flags
they do so for his benefit, and they reward him for his
obedience as if they had played no hand in bringing it
about. Yet with all that, there is something overvarnished
in this tale. The adult world (the teachers) is not that
benevolent, the citizenry (the peer-group) not that participative
