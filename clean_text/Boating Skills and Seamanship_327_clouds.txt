Clouds and Fronts

Three types of clouds accompany fronts—citrus,
stratus, and cumulus (Figure 12-11). All are the 
result of moisture-laden air carried aloft. As air rises,
it cools, and some of its moisture condenses to form
cloud. The rapidity with which warm, moist air
ascends determines the structure of the cloud. In
general, clouds get their names from their shapes
and their altitudes.
