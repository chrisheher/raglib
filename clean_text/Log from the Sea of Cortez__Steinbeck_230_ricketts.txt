How the business ran for twenty years no one knows, but it did
run even though it staggered a little sometimes. At times it would
spurt ahead with system and efficiency and then wearily collapse
for several months. Orders would pile up on the desk. Once during
a weary period someone sent Ed a cheesecake by parcel post. He
thought it was preserved material of some kind, and when he finally
opened it three months later we could not have identified it had it
not been that a note was enclosed which said, “Eat this cheesecake
at once. It’s very delicate.”
