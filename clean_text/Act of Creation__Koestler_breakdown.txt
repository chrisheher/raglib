To undo wrong connections, faulty integrations, is half the game. To
acquire a new habit is easy, because one main function of the nervous
system is to act as a habit-forming machine; to break out of a habit is
an almost heroic feat of mind or character. The prerequisite of originality 
is the art of forgetting, at the proper moment, what we know.
Hence, once more, the importance of the Unconscious—as an anaesthetist, 
who puts reason to sleep, and restores, for a transient moment,
the innocence of vision. Without the art of forgetting, the mind
