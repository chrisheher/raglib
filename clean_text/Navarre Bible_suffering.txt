consist in not feeling since that is proper to those
who have no feelings; nor does it lie in not

the law or obedience to God. For feeling is natural to the flesh, 
which is not like bronze; and so
reason does not remove it, because reason gives
to everything what its nature demands; and our
sensitivity is very soft and tender; when it 1s
wounded it of necessity feels, and when it feels
it has to cry out” (Fray Luis de Leén, Expositio
