The same approach can be used with couples who are
always fighting in an unproductive way. The therapist can
ask them to go home and have a fight at a set trme and for a

Similarly, if a child is provoking his parents, the therapist 
can ask him to do that for a full week. If he provokes
them, they will react differently because they have heard
him asked to do so. If he does not provoke them, the therapist
 has changed a pattern in the family, which is what he
is after.

To use the paradoxical approach a therapist must
develop skill and must practice. He also needs to be able
to think about problems in a gamelike or playful way even
though he realizes that he is dealing with grim problems and
real distress. He also needs to tolerate the emotional reaction 
of the family toward him, since this approach forces
them to deal with him in ways they have never dealt with a
helper before.

The design of a therapist’s directives in this approach
is relatively simple. He observes how the family members
deal with each other and directs them to behave in that way.
