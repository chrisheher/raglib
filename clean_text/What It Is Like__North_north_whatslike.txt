WHAT IT IS LIKE

Two pensive monkeys chained heartbreakingly to a metal ring
in a high, thick-walled window arch (no glazing) which looks
out behind on a pale harbor and filmy blue and green city
in the far distance. Clearly the monkeys are conscious; have
consciousness. We know what it is like for them to have given
up hope and to look only inward, while appearing to stare at the
ring imprisoning them and the space just below the window in
front, between it and us.
